The bottleneck
didn't disappear.
It moved online.

The shift to digital healthcare solved one problem and created another. Paper forms became digital forms. Filing cabinets became cloud folders. The bottleneck did not disappear — it just moved online.

For psychologists, nonprofits, and community organizations serving vulnerable populations, this bottleneck is acute. They manage hundreds of intake documents across multiple sources with no way to synthesize, analyze, or surface patterns. Mainstream AI tools that could help are off limits — sending sensitive client data to US-hosted platforms violates Alberta's Health Information Act and the trust of the populations they serve.

Insyte is a privacy-first document intelligence platform built for Canadian regulatory compliance. Clinicians upload intake forms, medical records, and assessments. Insyte returns a one-page synthesis, flags risk indicators, and detects patterns across documents. All data stays on Canadian soil.

We are not digitizing paperwork. We are making it intelligent.

The Problem

Digital storage was step one. Comprehension is step two — and no one has built it for Canada.

Organizations serving vulnerable populations made the switch from paper to digital. But the work of reading, organizing, and understanding documents remained entirely manual. And the AI tools that could change that are legally inaccessible to them.

Compliance Gap
01

A compliance gap no one has closed

Most AI tools in this space are HIPAA-compliant — a US standard. Organizations in Alberta operating under PIPA, PIPEDA, and the Health Information Act have no equivalent option that meets their legal obligations.

02

Documentation consumes the entire day

A typical nonprofit hires one or two intake staff whose entire shifts are spent on documentation and file processing. That time cannot be spent on the people who need help.

03

Existing systems only store, never understand

Current EHR tools file records but don't read them. Clinicians still do the organizing, pattern-finding, and summarizing manually — every single time.

04

People are falling through the cracks

When staff capacity is consumed by paperwork, timely support becomes impossible. For individuals relying on these services, delays are not inconveniences — they are crises.

The Solution

Document intelligence. Built for Canadian law, designed for clinical reality.

Insyte automates intake documentation and file comprehension — built from the ground up to meet Canadian and Albertan privacy requirements. Clinicians upload documents from multiple sources. Insyte reads, synthesizes, and surfaces what matters. No workarounds. No legal risk.

Multi-source document ingestion

Upload intake forms, assessments, and medical records from multiple sources and formats. Insyte processes and organizes them without manual sorting.

Synthesized one-page summaries

Every client file becomes a concise, structured summary — giving clinicians the full picture in seconds rather than hours.

Keyword search and pattern detection

Search across all documents instantly and surface patterns across files that would otherwise go unnoticed in a heavy caseload.

Sentiment analysis and risk flagging

Detect shifts in tone and flag potential risk indicators early — so no signal gets buried in administrative noise.
